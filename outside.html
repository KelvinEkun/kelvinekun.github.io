Outside the Lab

A PhD is not a personality. Here's what else is going on.

I grew up in Nigeria and spent a good chunk of my early career writing anything and everything — career guides, tech explainers, sustainability reports — before pivoting fully into research. That background shapes how I think: I care about clarity, about making ideas stick, and about the gap between what research says and what people actually understand.

Outside of optimization models and proofs, I follow football obsessively, read widely, and still write when the subject is interesting enough.

Football

I have been watching European football since I was a teenager in Ibadan. I am a Real Madrid fan who sometimes supports Manchester United — when they do not have managers like Amorim in charge.

I used to write about it too. Five published pieces on European football for CrossandNod, which mostly involved convincing an editor that process-based analysis was more interesting than match reports. I still think it is.

Hot take on record: Expected goals (xG) is the most useful thing football analytics gave the public, and most people still use it wrong. It describes finishing luck and has nothing to do with quality. The metric does not care who took the shot — be it Lewangoalski or Yakubu.
