About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Industrial Engineering and Operations Research at the University of Massachusetts Amherst, advised by Ana Muriel. I received my B.Sc. in Industrial and Production Engineering from the University of Ibadan.

My research sits at the intersection of machine learning and operations research, with a focus on practical decision-making models for make-to-order (MTO) manufacturing firms. My dissertation studies online lead-time quotation policies when customer responses are delayed, creating contingent demand and capacity outcomes.

More broadly, I am interested in online learning, revenue management, and primal-dual methods for large-scale optimization problems. I also have experience leading the technology development of a quotation-support tool for industrial partners.
